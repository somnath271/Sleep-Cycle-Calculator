In-Depth Guide to Sleep Cycles & Better Rest

Understanding sleep cycles is the first step to better sleep. This short guide explains how cycles work, practical tips to improve sleep quality, and how to use REMNIX to pick the best sleep schedule for your life.

What Are Sleep Cycles?

A typical sleep cycle lasts about 90 minutes and includes light sleep, deep (slow-wave) sleep, and REM sleep. Completing full cycles helps memory consolidation, emotional regulation, and physical recovery.

Optimizing the timing of your sleep (bedtime and wake time) so you wake at the end of a cycle reduces grogginess and improves daytime alertness.

How to Use This Calculator

  1. Choose either a desired wake time or how much sleep you want.
  2. We calculate multiple bedtimes that align with full 90-minute cycles.
  3. Pick a bedtime that fits your routine — earlier is usually better for consistent sleep health.

Tip: Allow ~14 minutes for typical sleep latency (time it takes to fall asleep) when planning your bedtime.

Quick UX Tips

  • Use dim lighting before bed.
  • Avoid caffeine late in the day.
  • Keep a consistent wake-up time.

Who benefits most?

Shift workers, students, and anyone with irregular sleep schedules can use cycle-aware timing to improve daytime function.

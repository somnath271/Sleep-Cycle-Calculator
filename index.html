In-Depth Guide to Sleep Cycles & Better Rest

Understanding sleep cycles is the first step to better sleep. This guide explains how cycles work, practical tips to improve sleep quality, and how to use REMNIX to pick the best sleep schedule for your life.

What Are Sleep Cycles?

A typical sleep cycle lasts about 90 minutes and includes light sleep (N1, N2), deep slow-wave sleep (N3), and REM sleep. Completing full cycles helps memory consolidation, emotional regulation, and physical recovery.

Timing your bedtime and wake time around complete cycles, so you wake during light sleep which reduces grogginess and improves daytime alertness.

How to Use This Calculator

  1. Choose either a desired wake time or how much sleep you want.
  2. We calculate multiple bedtimes that align with full 90-minute cycles.
  3. Pick a bedtime that fits your routine, earlier is usually better for consistent sleep health.

Tip: Allow ~14 minutes for typical sleep latency (the time it takes to fall asleep) when planning your bedtime.

Expert Sleep Tips

Keep a Consistent Schedule

Go to bed and wake at similar times daily to stabilise your circadian rhythm even on weekends.

Optimise Your Bedroom

Keep it cool (15-19°C), dark, and quiet. Limit screens 60-90 minutes before bed.

Relax Before Sleep

Try breathing exercises, light stretching, or a short reading routine to wind down before bed.

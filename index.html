Frequently Asked Questions

A sleep cycle is a period of sleep that typically lasts about 90 minutes and includes different stages of sleep, from light sleep to deep sleep and REM sleep. Completing full sleep cycles is important for feeling rested and refreshed.

Most adults need 4-6 complete sleep cycles per night, which translates to 6-9 hours of sleep. The exact number depends on your individual needs and lifestyle.

The best time to wake up is at the end of a sleep cycle, when you're in light sleep. Our calculator helps you determine the optimal wake-up time based on your sleep cycles.

Expert Sleep Tips

Consistent Sleep Schedule

Maintain a regular sleep schedule, even on weekends, to regulate your body's internal clock.

  • Go to bed at the same time each night
  • Wake up at the same time each morning
  • Avoid large variations in sleep times

Sleep Environment

Create an optimal sleep environment to promote better sleep quality.

  • Keep your bedroom cool and dark
  • Use comfortable bedding
  • Minimize noise and distractions

Pre-Sleep Routine

Develop a relaxing pre-sleep routine to signal your body it's time to sleep.

  • Avoid screens before bed
  • Practice relaxation techniques
  • Limit caffeine and heavy meals

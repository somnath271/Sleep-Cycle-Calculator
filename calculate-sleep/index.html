How to Improve Your Sleep Quality

High-quality sleep depends on consistent sleep schedules, a dark and cool sleep environment, and healthy sleep hygiene habits. Go to bed and wake up at the same time daily to reinforce your circadian rhythm. Avoid caffeine and heavy meals late in the day, and reduce exposure to blue light in the evening. Create a relaxing pre-sleep routine—reading, light stretching, or meditation—to signal to your body that it's time to wind down.

What Happens During Sleep

Sleep is composed of NREM (stages N1, N2, N3) and REM sleep, cycling every ~90 minutes. In N3 (deep sleep), the body focuses on physical recovery: tissue repair, immune support, and growth hormone release. In REM sleep, the brain consolidates memories, processes emotions, and supports learning and creativity. Cycling between these stages is normal and healthy.

Our sleep cycle article explains how your body moves through these stages and why timing your wake-up at the end of a cycle can help you feel more alert in the morning.

What is REM Sleep?

REM (Rapid Eye Movement) sleep is the stage associated with vivid dreams and heightened brain activity. It supports memory consolidation, emotional regulation, and neuroplasticity. Adults typically spend 20–25% of the night in REM, with longer REM periods occurring in the second half of the night. Disruptions to REM—like inconsistent bedtimes, alcohol, or stress—can affect mood, cognition, and next-day performance.

Tips to Fall Asleep Faster

  • Keep a consistent bedtime and wake time—even on weekends.
  • Dim lights 60–90 minutes before bed to support melatonin release.
  • Avoid caffeine after mid-day and limit late-night heavy meals.
  • Use a wind-down routine (reading, breathing exercises, light stretching).
  • Keep your bedroom cool, dark, and quiet; consider blackout curtains and white noise.
  • Reserve the bed for sleep and intimacy; avoid doomscrolling in bed.

Common Sleep Questions

How many hours of sleep do I need? Most adults need 7–9 hours per night, often corresponding to 5–6 sleep cycles. Teens and older adults have different ranges. If you wake groggy, try adjusting your sleep window by 15–30 minutes and aim to wake at the end of a 90-minute cycle.

What’s the best time to sleep? Align sleep with your circadian rhythm by keeping a consistent schedule. Use this bedtime calculator to find an optimal window based on your desired wake time or current bedtime.
